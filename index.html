What to Look For

Choosing the Right Tub for Your Life

Not every walk in tub is right for every person. The right tub depends on your body, your bathroom dimensions, and how you actually plan to use it. Here's what we focus on:

  • Step threshold height — ideally 2 to 4 inches for most mobility needs
  • Interior seat width and depth for comfortable, secure seating
  • Door opening width and swing direction relative to your space
  • Drain speed — fast-drain models empty in under 3 minutes
  • Grab bar placement and stability for safe transfers
  • Hydrotherapy options that match your health and comfort goals
  • Whether your plumbing and electrical can support the model you want
  • Installation method — one-day professional or DIY drop-ship
Chicago homes vary widely — bungalows, two-flats, condos. We verify clearance, comfort sizing, and plumbing fit before you commit to anything.

Fast Drain Technology

Don't Overlook Drain Speed

Most people focus on jets, seat size, and step height when choosing a walk in tub. Drain speed gets overlooked — until you're sitting in cooling water waiting to get out.

Because walk in tubs seal you inside, you must wait for the water to drain before opening the door. Standard models take 3 to 7 minutes. Fast-drain models with larger drain systems can empty in under 3 minutes. That might not sound like a big deal until you're sitting in a cold tub on a February morning in Chicago.

We always discuss drain speed during your consultation. It's one of the first things we ask about because it directly affects daily comfort. Fast-drain is worth the investment for most people — and we'll tell you which models have it.

Types of Walk In Tubs

Find the Right Style for Your Needs

Walk in tubs aren't one-size-fits-all. The right model depends on your health goals, bathroom layout, and how you enjoy bathing.

Most Popular

Soaker Walk In Tub

No jets, just warm water immersion with a safe entry door and low step threshold. Simple to use, easy to maintain, and the most affordable. Perfect for seniors who want a safer, more relaxing bath without complicated features.

Most Therapeutic

Hydrotherapy Tub

Adds air jets, water jets, or both. Air jets create a gentle bubbly massage. Water jets provide targeted, deeper muscle relief. Great for arthritis, back pain, and poor circulation. In-line heaters keep water warm during long soaks.

Best of Both

Combination Dual Tub

Can't decide between air jets and water jets? You don't have to. Combination tubs include both systems. Ideal for users with multiple health concerns who want maximum flexibility every time they bathe.

For Walk-Ins

Walk In Shower Combo

Some models include an overhead shower system, giving you both functions in one unit. A popular option for Chicago condos and smaller bathrooms where space is limited but both a shower and tub are desired.

Wheelchair Users

Roll-In Shower Option

If a tub isn't the right fit, a roll-in accessible shower may be a better solution. Barrier-free entry, grab bars, fold-down seating, and handheld showerheads. We help you compare both options honestly — no agenda.

Larger Bodies

Bariatric & Wide Models

Standard walk in tubs may feel cramped for taller or broader individuals. Bariatric models offer larger interior dimensions, wider door openings, and reinforced seating. A tub that fits your body is always the right tub.
